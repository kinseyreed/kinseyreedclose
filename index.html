About Me

Bridging ecology and agronomy for healthier soils

I'm a soil scientist and microbial ecologist studying how plant and microbial biodiversity shape soil health in Appalachian agroecosystems. I completed my PhD in Plant & Soil Science (with an emphasis in Applied & Environmental Microbiology) at West Virginia University in 2025, and I now work as a postdoctoral researcher in the Morrissey Microbial Ecology, Biogeochemistry & Soil Health Lab.

My research connects the organisms beneath our feet — bacteria, fungi, and the plants they partner with — to the practical questions farmers face: how do we build soil carbon, keep nutrients in the ground, and graze pastures sustainably? I work directly with Appalachian producers through the GRASS project and USDA-funded soil health trials, translating microbial ecology into on-the-farm practice.

What I Do

Research focus

Understanding the living soil — and putting that knowledge to work for farmers and the land.

01

Microbes & Soil Carbon

Using quantitative stable isotope probing to link microbial biodiversity to nitrogen cycling, carbon stabilization, and soil aggregate stability.

02

Pasture Biodiversity

Characterizing plant community composition and structure across central Appalachian pastures and how diversity drives soil health.

03

Farmer Partnerships

Co-designing soil health demonstration trials with Appalachian producers to make grazing more sustainable and carbon-smart.

GRASS Project

GRazing Appalachia SuStainably — working directly with 135 farmers to adopt climate-smart, soil-building grazing practices across the region. I serve as Soil Health Specialist and interim Program Coordinator through WVU Extension's Small Farms Center.

USDA NRCS CIG · 2022–2026 · $1.0M

Diversifying Appalachia's Pastures

A Conservation Innovation Grant soil health demonstration trial testing how pasture plant diversity improves soil health and carbon storage across 17 working farms in West Virginia.
