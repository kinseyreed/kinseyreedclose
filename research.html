Research & Publications

The living soil, from microbe to pasture

My work integrates microbial ecology, plant community ecology, and biogeochemistry to understand and improve soil health in working Appalachian agroecosystems.

Microbial Ecology & Isotopes

Advancing field- and lab-based quantitative stable isotope probing (qSIP) to identify the microbes driving nitrogen assimilation, carbon cycling, and soil organic matter formation.

Soil Health & Aggregate Stability

Linking actinobacteria, mycorrhizae, and microbial interactions to the physical stability of soil aggregates and long-term carbon stabilization.

Pasture Diversity & Grazing

Quantifying pasture plant community composition and structure, and testing how diversifying pastures improves soil health and livestock-production sustainability.
